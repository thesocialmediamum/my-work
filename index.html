Case Study 01

Caribbean Café and Restaurant Launch (UK)

Problem

An existing client who owns a UK based hair salon I was already managing on social opened a second business, a Caribbean café and restaurant. She reached out close to opening day with no content strategy in place. She had a social account for the café but it was dormant. The challenge was driving opening day traffic and building awareness for the new spot with very little lead time.

Solution

I used a borrowed audience strategy for opening day. Since I was already managing the salon and understood that the salon and the Caribbean café had overlapping audiences, the same UK based Caribbean community, I leveraged the salon's existing audience to drive opening day traffic to the café. I also did competitor research and saw that no other Caribbean cafés in her area were leveraging social proof and customer reactions the way I planned to. This gave the launch a clear edge.

Implementation

Took over the dormant café account and started posting consistently from opening day. Cross promoted from the salon page to the café page on opening day to drive the warm audience over. After that, the growth engine became social proof. Told the owner to film customer reactions throughout opening day and beyond, people tasting the food for the first time, the queue, the energy in the room. Used those clips as Reels and Stories with captions written in the way her customers actually speak. Kept the posting rhythm tight in the first weeks while interest was hottest, so the algorithm and the audience both stayed locked in.

Outcome

The page grew from 236 followers to 647 followers in weeks, a 174% increase with zero ad spend. Foot traffic increased and the brand now takes orders online daily, with customers functioning as the primary marketing engine.

Case Study 02

Personal Brand from Zero

Problem

Small business owners across Africa were underserved by the marketing content available to them. Existing advice was either too jargon heavy, too platform generic, or written for audiences outside their commercial reality. There was a clear positioning gap in the market for a trusted voice translating marketing strategy into the everyday context of African small business.

Solution

Built TheSocialMediaMum as a content engine designed to fill that positioning gap. Stripped out jargon, anchored every piece of content in real, recurring problems the audience faced, and built the brand as the bridge between professional marketing thinking and the lived reality of running a small business in Africa.

Implementation

Defined three core content pillars, organic growth, content strategy, and business clarity. Built a weekly content rhythm of short form video on Instagram and TikTok, long form carousels, and live training. Used real client stories and personal experience as the spine of every piece. Distributed across Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, and a private WhatsApp community. Layered paid training products on top of the content engine so the audience had a clear path from free content to monetised learning.

Outcome

Grew to 50,000+ followers organically across Instagram and TikTok, reached 2.3 million profile views in a single month, trained over 700 entrepreneurs through paid programmes, secured paid brand partnerships with Zoho, Fidelity Bank and others, and was featured in Vanguard Nigeria, This Day Live and Times Square New York.

Case Study 03

African Food Retail Brand Launch (Dallas, USA)

Problem

A new African food retail brand was preparing to launch in Dallas with no existing audience, no community presence, and a customer base that needed to be built before the doors even opened. The challenge was creating demand and emotional investment in a brand that did not yet exist commercially.

Solution

Built the full pre-launch and launch content strategy from scratch. The approach was rooted in storytelling and building in public, behind the scenes content, countdowns, and community-focused posts that brought the Dallas diaspora community into the journey from day one. Every piece of content was designed to make the audience feel like they were part of building something, not being sold to.

Implementation

Designed a 6 week pre-launch content runway with weekly milestones. Built brand voice around warmth, familiarity, and shared cultural memory for the Nigerian diaspora in Dallas. Produced short form video showing African food prep, packaging, and the founder story. Wrote captions that triggered shares from people tagging family members who would recognize the products. Coordinated launch day content live from the store. Held a weekly content meeting with the owner to align on product priorities and seasonal pushes.

Outcome

Grew the brand entirely organically before launch with zero paid advertising, multiple videos went viral during the pre-launch phase, creating a fully primed audience by launch day. Generated 188,000 account views, 3,993 profile visits, and 708 external link taps in a single month. The launch was a success and the brand has since grown to over 6,900 followers. I continue to manage their content on retainer today.

— Busolami Sokunbi-Jones, CEO, Mamajones African Market (Dallas, Texas)

Project 01

The 30 Day Reel Challenge

Problem

Small business owners across Africa wanted to grow on social media but were stuck in an inconsistency loop. They understood short form video was the fastest growth lever but had no system, no creator habit, and no accountability layer to actually ship daily content.

Solution

Designed a paid 30 day reel challenge that combined daily content prompts, training, and peer accountability inside a private community. The product was built around behaviour change, not just education. Build the creator habit, master short form video, and exit with 30 reels plus measurable organic growth.

Outcome

Over 700 business owners trained across multiple cohorts. The challenge became the flagship training product of TheSocialMediaMum and continues to drive recurring paid sign ups, brand authority, and a steady pipeline of testimonial and case study material.

Project 02

Reelers Hub WhatsApp Community

Problem

Students who completed the 30 Day Reel Challenge needed a retention layer to sustain momentum after the cohort ended. Instagram alone could not host the kind of ongoing peer feedback, accountability, and depth of conversation that drives long term behaviour change.

Solution

Built Reelers Hub, a private WhatsApp community designed as the engagement and retention layer beneath the brand. The space functions as both a peer support network and a live audience research channel, generating real time signal on what small business owners are struggling with, what language they use, and what content actually resonates.

Outcome

Scaled the community to 329 active small business owners. The hub now feeds directly into content strategy, product development, and audience insight, closing the loop between training, retention, and ongoing brand growth.
